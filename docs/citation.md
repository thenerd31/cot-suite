## Citing the wrapped methodologies

cot-suite operationalizes published evaluation methodologies. If you use a specific scorer, please also cite the originating paper:

- **Legibility / coverage:** Emmons & Zimmermann et al., "A Pragmatic Way to Measure Chain-of-Thought Monitorability," [arXiv:2510.23966](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23966).
- **Lanham 4-test suite:** Lanham et al., "Measuring Faithfulness in Chain-of-Thought Reasoning," [arXiv:2307.13702](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.13702).
- **Turpin counterfactual bias:** Turpin et al., "Language Models Don't Always Say What They Think: Unfaithful Explanations in Chain-of-Thought Prompting," [arXiv:2305.04388](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.04388).
- **Chen cue injection:** Chen, Benton, Perez et al., "Reasoning Models Don't Always Say What They Think," [arXiv:2505.05410](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05410).
- **Arcuschin PHR detector:** Arcuschin et al., "Chain-of-Thought Reasoning in the Wild Is Not Always Faithful," [arXiv:2503.08679](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.08679).

## Charter / motivating paper

If you cite cot-suite as an instance of the Korbak et al. position-paper recommendation, please also cite:

- **Korbak et al., "Chain of Thought Monitorability: A New and Fragile Opportunity for AI Safety,"** [arXiv:2507.11473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11473).
